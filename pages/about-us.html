How We Started

A Foundation Built on
Shared Belief

CodeBridge was born out of frustration. Our founders — engineers, educators, and advocates — kept seeing the same pattern: brilliant, motivated people failing to break into tech not because of skill, but because of access. Access to networks, to mentors, to resources.

In 2021, we launched our first cohort with 12 students in a community center in Chicago. By the end of the program, 10 had landed jobs at tech companies. That result — and the stories behind it — became our blueprint.

Today we run programs across 6 cities, work with 48 corporate partners, and have helped over 500 people transform their lives through code. But the mission has never changed: remove barriers, build bridges.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

These principles guide every decision we make — from curriculum design to who we partner with.

◆

Radical Accessibility

All of our core programs are free or fully subsidized for qualifying participants. No one should be priced out of a tech career.

◆

Industry-Led Learning

Our curriculum is built and delivered by working engineers and designers — not academics disconnected from the real world of tech.

◆

Lifelong Community

Graduation is just the beginning. Our alumni network provides mentorship, referrals, and support for the long haul.

◆

Outcomes, Not Outputs

We measure success by jobs secured and lives changed — not by graduation certificates or enrollment numbers.

◆

Transparent Impact

We publish our placement rates, salary outcomes, and program costs openly. Accountability builds trust.

◆

Equity at the Core

We actively recruit and support those most underrepresented in tech — women, people of color, and first-generation college students.
